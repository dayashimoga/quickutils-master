# Why Your Insurance Premium Keeps Rising: Factors You Can Control

Every year, millions of policyholders see their insurance premiums increase without fully understanding why. While some factors are beyond your control (like age), many premium determinants are within your power to influence. Understanding what drives premiums helps you make smarter insurance decisions and potentially save thousands annually.

## How Insurance Premiums Are Calculated

Insurance companies use actuarial science to price risk. Your premium is essentially the insurer's estimate of how likely you are to file a claim, multiplied by the potential claim amount, plus the company's operating costs and profit margin.

The basic formula: **Premium = (Risk Probability × Expected Claim Amount) + Administrative Costs + Profit Margin**

For health insurance, a 25-year-old non-smoker in good health might have a 2% annual claim probability, while a 55-year-old with pre-existing conditions might have a 15% probability. This explains the dramatic price difference between age groups.

## The Age Factor: What You Can't Change

Age is the single largest premium determinant across all insurance types. Here's how it typically affects premiums:

| Age Group | Health Premium Multiplier | Life Premium Multiplier |
|-----------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| 18-25 | 1.0× (baseline) | 1.0× (baseline) |
| 26-35 | 1.2-1.5× | 1.1-1.3× |
| 36-45 | 1.8-2.5× | 1.5-2.0× |
| 46-55 | 3.0-4.0× | 2.5-3.5× |
| 56-65 | 4.5-6.0× | 4.0-6.0× |

**Key insight**: This is why financial advisors universally recommend buying life and health insurance early — a policy purchased at 25 locks in lower base rates for life (in the case of term insurance) or establishes a claims history that keeps renewals affordable.

## 5 Factors You CAN Control

### 1. Your BMI and Health Metrics

Many insurers now offer wellness discounts for policyholders who maintain healthy BMI (18.5-24.9), non-smoker status, and regular health checkups. Some modern health insurance plans offer 5-15% premium discounts for verified fitness milestones.

Use our [free BMI Calculator](/tools.html#bmi-calculator) to check if you're in the healthy range that qualifies for lower premiums.

### 2. Coverage Amount and Deductible Balance

Choosing the right coverage amount is crucial — over-insuring wastes money while under-insuring creates dangerous gaps. The optimal coverage depends on your specific situation:

- **Health Insurance**: 5-10× your annual income is a common recommendation
- **Life Insurance**: 10-15× annual income minus existing assets
- **Auto Insurance**: Third-party liability is mandatory; comprehensive coverage should match your vehicle's current market value, not its showroom price

Increasing your deductible (the amount you pay before insurance kicks in) can significantly reduce premiums. Raising a health insurance deductible from ₹10,000 to ₹50,000 might reduce your premium by 20-30%.

### 3. No-Claim Bonus Preservation

Most health and auto insurance policies offer no-claim bonuses (NCB) — premium discounts for years without claims. After 5 consecutive claim-free years, NCB can reduce your premium by 50% or more. Avoid filing small claims that cost less than your NCB value. If your NCB saves you ₹8,000 annually and a small claim is worth ₹5,000, paying out of pocket is the better financial decision.

### 4. Policy Bundling and Loyalty

Bundle your insurance products (health + auto + life) with the same insurer for 5-15% multi-policy discounts. Long-term loyalty with one insurer often results in preferential renewal rates and faster claim settlements.

### 5. Annual vs. Monthly Payment

Paying premiums annually instead of monthly saves 3-8% on most policies. This is because insurers charge a convenience fee for installment processing, and annual payers eliminate the insurer's collection risk.

## When to Switch Insurance Providers

Consider switching if:
- Your current premium has increased by more than 15% without corresponding benefit improvements
- You find a comparable plan with 20%+ lower premiums from a reputable insurer
- Your claim settlement ratio has been poor (below 90%)

Before switching, check the new insurer's claim settlement ratio (available on IRDAI's website), network hospitals, and waiting period policies for pre-existing conditions.

## The Bottom Line

While age-related premium increases are inevitable, proactive health management, smart coverage selection, NCB preservation, and strategic payment timing can keep your premiums manageable.
